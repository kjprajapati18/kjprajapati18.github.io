Variables


What are Variables?

Variables are symbols used to represent numbers in algebraic equations and expressions. They are at the heart of basically all Algebra topics and are consistently used in higher levels of mathematics. Up until now, you have probably used variables without even realizing it! Whenever we do not know the value of something, we represent its value with a symbol (most commonly "x"). Learning solve single variable equations and how to translate word problems into variable expressions is crucial towards understanding future topics.

Examples of Variables:

We'll start off with some very simple examples of variables. You may have previously seen boxes, question marks, smiley faces, or other symbols used in place of variables. Variables are a more formal way of writing these expressions or equations. Instead of using these somewhat complex symbols (especially smiley faces), mathmaticians use letters in the English or Greek alphabet. The most common variable is X, but you are allowed to use any letter of your choice.

1. X - 2 = 0


2. 4Y - 2 = 10


3. 6Z - 26 > 10
